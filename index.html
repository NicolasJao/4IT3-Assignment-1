Biography

"We spend our entire lives trying to tell stories about ourselves."

-Ken Liu, author of The Paper Menagerie and Other Stories

Nicolas Jao

I was born in the Philippines and grew up in Aurora, Ontario, but I also have Chinese ancestry. I am currently studying at McMaster University in Hamilton, in a specialized automation engineering co-op program. I have a ton of family (you know us Filipinos) and I do anything and everything with mine. I'm doing my best to make them proud!


I am passionate about writing fiction in the form of short stories and novels (check out my portfolio), and naturally that makes me a big reader as well. I love eating food, watching movies, basketball, playing the piano, learning card magic to impress my friends, and I am a massive dog person despite not having a dog (the obsession is out of hand at this point).


I'm a huge nerd about everything! In my spare time or while I'm eating my meals, you'll likely find me watching YouTube videos about anything from geography, history, philosophy, economics, technology, or even quantum physics. Funny, I know. Learning about the world is my lifetime hobby and I want to know of everything that exists to broaden my global perspective. I'll often explain niche things about a specific culture or a relatively unknown scientific fact to my friends (leaving them amazed hopefully, but more often than not, confused).


Despite wanting to be a world-renowned author as a kid, I've entered the world of engineering. I picked up solid programming skills from high school, CAD and electrical skills from an FRC team, and I know and hang out with a lot of cool and nerdy people. I've always found robots to be awesome (who doesn't, really), and designing them for a career was something I was always sure of about myself. Recently though, I've been learning about PLCs and the automation industry in my classes and have been getting heavily interested in that as well. Perhaps doing something related to both, such as being a controls programmer for industrial manufacturing robots and PLCs, would be the best way to describe my dream job. Especially one that sends me to different countries, wherever our customers are, to travel the world.


I enjoy meeting similar people and hearing new perspectives. Feel free to contact me to talk about the tech industry, the next year the Toronto Raptors are going to win the championship, or to send me a video of a funny dog.
